<Section icon={Shield} title="Maximizing Profit and Managing Commissions">

Understanding the math behind **sell your avatar clothing sales** is essential for long-term growth. Roblox's economy involves several layers of "taxes" and commissions that can eat into your margins if you aren't careful.

When a user buys your outfit, the Robux is distributed among the creators of the individual items. If you designed the shirt (costing 5 Robux), you receive 70% of that price (3.5 Robux, rounded down). If you also "referred" the sale of a UGC hat (costing 100 Robux), you may receive a 10-40% affiliate commission depending on the platform used for the sale.

| Item Category | Sale Price (Avg) | Your Profit (Creator) | Your Profit (Affiliate) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Classic Shirt** | 5 Robux | 3 Robux | N/A |
| **UGC Accessory** | 50-100 Robux | N/A | 10-40 Robux |
| **Full Bundle** | 250+ Robux | 3-10 Robux | 25-100 Robux |

By focusing on high-priced UGC items within your curated looks, your affiliate commissions will often far outweigh the direct profit from your own clothing designs. This is why "Avatar Styling" has become a legitimate career path within the Roblox community in 2026.

<Callout type="info" title="Revenue Note">
Robux earned from sales usually enters a "Pending" state for 3 to 7 days before being credited to your balance. This is a standard security measure to prevent fraudulent transactions.
</Callout>

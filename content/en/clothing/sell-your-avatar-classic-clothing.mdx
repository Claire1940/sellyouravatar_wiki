<Section icon={Map} title="Step-by-Step Uploading Process">

Publishing your designs to the Roblox platform requires navigating the Creator Dashboard. You must ensure that your templates are perfectly aligned to avoid texture seams or rejection during the moderation process. Before uploading, it is highly recommended to test your designs in Roblox Studio using a dummy rig to check for alignment issues.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Design the Template">
    Download the official Roblox classic clothing template and use a photo editor to paint your design onto the designated sections.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Access the Dashboard">
    Navigate to the Roblox Creator Dashboard, select the "Development Items" tab, and choose the classic clothing category.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Upload the File">
    Choose the correct category (Shirts or Pants), select your saved template file, name your item, and pay the 10 Robux fee.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Configure for Sale">
    Open the uploaded item settings, enable the "On Sale" toggle, set your desired Robux price, and save the changes.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Testing your clothing in a local Roblox Studio environment saves you money and time. If you upload a template with alignment errors, you will have to pay the 10 Robux fee again to upload the corrected version. Use the "Rig Builder" plugin in Roblox Studio, insert a blocky R15 rig, and apply your clothing ID to preview how the seams connect at the shoulders, torso, and legs.

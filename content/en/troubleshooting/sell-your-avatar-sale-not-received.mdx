The most common reason for missing funds is the Roblox escrow system. Whenever an item or outfit is sold, Roblox holds the Robux in a pending state to prevent fraud, unauthorized transactions, and double-spending. This safety measure ensures that both the buyer and the seller are protected. During high server load or site-wide updates, these transaction records can take longer than usual to appear in your account summary.

| Status | Description | Typical Duration | Action Required |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Pending | Robux is held by Roblox for safety verification | 3 to 7 days | Wait for escrow to clear |
| Success | Transaction complete and Robux added to balance | Instant after escrow | None |
| Failed | Transaction cancelled due to payment issues | Immediate | Re-verify buyer payment |
| Delayed | High server load delaying transaction logs | 12 to 24 hours | Check status page |

<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Troubleshooting for Missing Sales">

If you are confident that a sale occurred but your balance has not updated, follow these troubleshooting steps to locate your funds and verify the transaction status.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Access Roblox Transactions">
    Log into your Roblox account via a web browser. Navigate to the Trade tab on the left sidebar and click on the Summary option to view your pending sales.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Check Pending Robux">
    Look at the Pending Robux section. If your sale was registered, the Robux will be listed here. This balance is separate from your active spending balance.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Verify Outfit Asset Settings">
    Go to your inventory and select the UGC item or T-shirt used in the outfit. Ensure the item is configured as "On Sale" and has a valid price set.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Confirm Community Outfit Status">
    If you used Catalog Avatar Creator, open the game and check the Community Outfits tab. Confirm that your outfit has not been moderated or taken down.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Section icon={Target} title="Troubleshooting Step-by-Step">

If you are consistently seeing the **sell your avatar upload failed** message, follow this systematic approach to identify and resolve the bottleneck. Most issues are related to how the game reads your current character's inventory.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Verify Asset Ownership">
    Ensure every item on your avatar is currently in your inventory. If you are wearing a "Try-on" item from a shop that you haven't purchased yet, the upload will fail.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Check Creator Permissions">
    Open your Roblox dashboard and confirm that the shirt or UGC item you intend to profit from is set to "Public" and "On Sale." If the item is private, the game cannot link it to a transaction.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Simplify the Outfit">
    Remove complex accessories, animations, or emotes. Try uploading a "Basic" version of the outfit with just a shirt and pants. If this works, add accessories back one by one to find the culprit.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Refresh Session">
    Log out of Roblox, clear your browser or app cache, and log back in. This forces the game to re-sync your inventory data with the cloud servers.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Validation Check">
Before clicking upload, click the "Try-ons" tab in your avatar editor. If any item shows a price but doesn't have an "Owned" checkmark, buy it or remove it.
</Callout>

</Section>
